Reviews/Summaries :-

  1. Deadpool (Movie):

    "Deadpool" is a groundbreaking superhero film that defies the traditional genre expectations. Directed by Tim Miller and brilliantly brought to life by Ryan Reynolds in the titular role, the movie introduces audiences to Wade Wilson, a former Special Forces operative who becomes the antihero known as Deadpool after undergoing an experimental treatment for terminal cancer. The treatment leaves him with accelerated healing powers but also disfigures his body, igniting his quest for revenge against the man who ruined his life.

  2. Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (Book):

    "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone," written by J.K. Rowling, follows the journey of an eleven-year-old boy, Harry Potter, who discovers that he is a wizard. After growing up with his neglectful relatives, the Dursleys, Harry receives an acceptance letter to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. There, he learns about magic, makes close friends, and uncovers the truth about his past and the dark wizard Voldemort, who killed his parents.

  3. Squid game (TV Show):

    "Squid Game" is a South Korean survival drama series created by Hwang Dong-hyuk and released on Netflix in 2021. The story follows 456 financially distressed individuals who are invited to participate in a mysterious competition with a tempting prize of 45.6 billion won.
